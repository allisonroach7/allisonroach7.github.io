Our Story

From service to craftsmanship - the journey of two Marines creating art with purpose

Meet the Founders

Sean and Julie Lawrence - Veterans, artisans, and the heart behind Second Beat Studios

Sean Lawrence

Co-Founder & Master Craftsman

Sean served 8 years in the Marine Corps as a combat engineer, where he developed his precision craftsmanship skills. After transitioning to civilian life, he channeled his discipline and attention to detail into woodworking and instrument restoration.

US Marine Corps Veteran | 2008-2016

Julie Lawrence

Co-Founder & Creative Director

Julie served as a Marine Corps musician, playing trumpet in the Marine Band. Her musical background and artistic vision guide the transformation of each instrument into functional art that honors its musical heritage.

US Marine Corps Veteran | 2010-2018

Our Journey

How two Marines found a new mission in preserving musical history

2014

Meeting in the Corps

Sean and Julie met while stationed at Camp Lejeune, bonding over their shared love of music and craftsmanship during off-duty hours.

2016

First Upcycling Project

Sean transformed a damaged family heirloom violin into a beautiful wall clock as a wedding gift for Julie, discovering his passion for instrument upcycling.

2018

Transition to Civilian Life

After honorable discharges from the Marines, the couple settled in Nashville and began collecting broken instruments from local music shops.

2019

First Market Success

Their upcycled guitar lamps sold out at a local artisan market, confirming there was demand for their unique creations.

2020

Second Beat Studios Born

Officially established their LLC and opened a small workshop, combining their military discipline with artistic vision.

Present

Thriving Business

Now operating a successful studio with national clients, while maintaining their commitment to honoring each instrument's history.

Our Mission

To honor the service of musical instruments by giving them a second life, just as we were given new purpose after our military service. We approach each piece with the same discipline, respect, and attention to detail we learned in the Marines, creating functional art that tells a story.
